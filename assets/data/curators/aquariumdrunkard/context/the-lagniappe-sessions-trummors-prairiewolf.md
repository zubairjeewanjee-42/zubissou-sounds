## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

A meeting of the minds. Last year saw Trummors lit out for the territories touring some esoteric, off-the-beaten path venues in Colorado and New Mexico with Prairiewolf. This installment of the Lagniappe Sessions commemorates the experience with the following collaboration of the two bands reimagining early ’80s George Strait along with a Joni Mitchell chestnut. Trummors’ David Lerner’s notes on the session, below.

Trummors/Prairiewolf :: The Cowboy Rides Away (George Strait)

On our latest LP, we covered George Strait’s “I Can Still Make Cheyenne.” It was fun and got a warm reception, so we figured why not do one more? I suggested “The Cowboy Rides Away” after hearing a cover by a New Mexico group (whose name escapes me) on our local country station. That version used an analog 80s drum machine, which I thought sounded odd but cool, and then realized using a drum machine would benefit this collaboration, as we’d be recording sans live drums in our newly-assembled home studio, The Rainbow Room.  

Trummors/Prairiewolf :: Both Sides Now (Joni Mitchell)

“Both Sides Now” is a longtime favorite song lyrically and melodically, and also one of Joni Mitchell’s less intimidating to learn tunes. We thought Joni and George would be a sweet, if unexpected pairing. Arrangement-wise, I was inspired by Chet Atkins’ solo version for nylon string guitar, as performed on The Tonight Show in 1974. To record Anne’s vocals, we took a field trip to Jeremy from Prairiewolf’s lovely pad in Nederland, Colorado, right near the former site of the famous Caribou Ranch recording studio. (“The Taos to Nederland express train: the future liberals want,” as Jeremy put it.) While tracking, we were treated to a visitation from a family of moose. They were listening attentively at the window—one even made eye contact with Anne while she was singing, which we took as a good sign that the take was a keeper.

Credits: Stefan Beck: Lap steel. Anne Cunningham: Vocals, harmonium, feedback. Jeremy Erwin: Mellotron. David Lerner: Acoustic guitar, vocals. Tyler Wilcox: Bass. Photo: Michelle Martin.
