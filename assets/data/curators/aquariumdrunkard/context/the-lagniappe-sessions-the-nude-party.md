## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

The Nude Party return this month with the release of their latest—and fourth—LP, Look Who’s Back. Produced by Michael Rault in the desert at his studio in Joshua Tree, the seven-piece outfit boots up, gets loose, and locks in over the course of the record’s nine tracks. Keeping with the vibe, this installment of the Lagniappe Sessions catches up with the band as they pay tribute to 3‑Track Shack era, North Carolina godhead Link Wray, the mutable, perennial gem that is Chuck Willis’s “C.C. Rider,” and the road-worn groove of “Six Days on the Road.”

The Nude Party :: C.C. Rider (Chuck Willis)

We chose to cover this tune when we were deep in our Animals phase, from which we heard their version on the album Animalization. For a young college kid at the time, this song brought out whatever budding bluesman fantasy of myself was inside of me. Anytime we played New Orleans, Memphis, St. Louis, Chicago, or any place with a strong heritage of the blues etc., I fought for this song to be on the set list to prove our authenticity to the gods whom I believed were watching over us. I also believe that this tune was what won over the approval of Oakley Munson, our best friend, producer, and mentor. This was recorded in the basement of his old Asheville, NC house in the wee hours of the morning. 

The Nude Party :: Fallin’ Rain (Link Wray)

This song is pure NC. As a North Carolina native, Link Wray inspired a new world of guitar playing for me, and taught me that less is more and attitude is more important than ability (and at the time of our band’s beginning, we didn’t have much ability). This song is one of his more somber tunes, which also showcases the yin to his rock and roll yang. Also produced by Oakley, this was recorded in the basement of his home in the Catskills while 11 people occupied it during Covid. 

The Nude Party :: Six Days on the Road (Earl Green and Carl Montgomery)

When I first heard this song covered by Gram Parsons & The Fallen Angels, I knew we had to cover it. During the recording of our first record in 2018, I was obsessed with The Fallen Angels. They were and still are everything that combines rock and roll and authentic country music to me. This particular track was taken from the board live in Washington D.C. Our friend Haylie Davis (Lady Apple Tree) was on tour with us at the time and was kind enough to lend her incredible vocals to complete the full Emmylou experience.
