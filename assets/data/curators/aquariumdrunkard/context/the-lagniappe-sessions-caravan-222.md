## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

 “…I’m your toy, I’m your old boy.” Country-fried Los Angeles outfit Caravan 222 continue to put the honk in the tonk. For their Lagniappe Session, the septet works up stalwart ’70s British pub-rockers Brinsley Schwarz’s “Country Girl,” Danny O’Keefe’s 1972 chestnut “I’m Sober Now,” and a faithful rendition of Gram’s “Hot Burrito #1.” The band’s notes on their selections, below. 

Caravan 222 :: Country Girl (Brinsley Schwarz)

If you’re new to the Nick Lowe pub rock era, Brinsley Schwarz is the locale to start. While “Nervous On The Road” was a close runner up for this session, we opted for the simple groove of ol’ trusty “Country Girl.” The chorus gang vocals were done by local buds Cale Tyson and Hamilton Boyce — check them on the L.A. country scene. 

Caravan 222 :: I’m Sober Now (Danny O’Keefe)

We first played this tune for a High Country 4/20 set at Gold Diggers in East Hollywood this past year. We’re not too sure how sober Danny O’Keefe truly was, so we tend to nudge a couple shots towards our vocalist Valentino before this subdued burner. 

Caravan 222 :: Hot Burrito # 1 (The Flying Burrito Brothers)

All you Grampires out there know this is the high water mark in the Burrito/Parsons catalogue. Arguably, this is the all time most celebrated vocal take from Gram — vulnerable, tired, cracking, and helpless. To capture this one, we endured hours of relentless crying before we rolled tape. Done normally as the closer to a rowdy set, we do this one with genuine sincerity every time.

All songs recorded at Hound Dog Studios in Glassell Park with Jacob Johnston and mastered by John at Golden Mastering. Photo by Daniel Tierney on Caravan’s 1973 Cadillac Eldorado near Dodger Stadium.
