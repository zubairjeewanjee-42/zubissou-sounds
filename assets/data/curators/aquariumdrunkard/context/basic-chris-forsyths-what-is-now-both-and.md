## Body

Over the past 20 years or so, Chris Forsyth has proven himself to be one of our very best guitarists, a player whose abundant technical ability never gets in the way of a great riff, whose improvisational instincts never overwhelm his overall focus. He’s also not afraid of shaking things up, finding new contexts to keep his creativity fresh. To wit, Chris has two records with two bands this year — Basic’s self-titled second LP and the debut of WHAT IS NOW.

Basic is the trio’s first full-length featuring the mighty Douglas McCombs, following up the excellent Dream City EP from 2025. McCombs is an inspired addition to the trio; his Fender Bass VI sound is at this point as iconic as Neil Young’s Old Black — instantly recognizable, endlessly enjoyable. And he sounds amazing in the Basic setting, weaving effortlessly into Forsyth’s wiry guitar as it darts around over Mikel Patrick Avery’s ingenious electro-acoustic percussion blend. Avery, who also plays in Natural Information Society, is a supremely inventive musician — he’s a metronome but he’s also somehow deeply human, making repetition into something magical, layers upon layers, wheels within wheels. Forsyth revels in the inspiration and camaraderie that McCombs and Avery provide, tearing off spiky solos or simply getting into the groove and letting it ride.

WHAT IS NOW’s Both/And, released earlier in 2026, also lets it ride — but in a pretty different way. Here, Forsyth is joined by drummer Joey Sullivan (Florry, Bark Culture) and double bassist John Moran (Bark Culture, the Daniel Villareal Trio) for a pleasingly thorny set of improvs. This stuff is exploratory and expansive, the three musicians pulling the notes apart and pasting them back together into weird, thrilling shapes in real time. They’re in no hurry to get to where they’re going — the shortest of the album’s three tracks clocks in at just under 20 minutes. In other words, Both/And is an album that rewards listeners who are patient, open and ready for an adventure. That’s you, right? | t wilcox
