Shot for French television in Saint-Tropez during the summer of 1970, this broadcast captures Pink Floyd before the lore and iconography fully set in: no inflatable pigs, no circular screens, no stadium-scale spectacle, just four guys on a small stage dragging enormous sound through the Riviera night. What stands out now is the physicality of the set: Mason and Waters in lockstep repetition while Gilmour and Wright push the pieces outward in slow increments. Fifty-five years on, nothing here suggests future rock mythology; they still look like a working band loading in and setting up, years before the machinery of Pink Floyd became larger than the four people onstage.
