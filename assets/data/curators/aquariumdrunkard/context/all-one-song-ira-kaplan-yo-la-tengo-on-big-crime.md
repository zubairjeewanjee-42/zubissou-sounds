## Body

Hello again! Welcome back to All One Song, a Neil Young podcast presented by Aquarium Drunkard Transmissions. Last summer, we spent a couple months talking to some of our favorite musicians, writers and artists about their favorite Neil Young songs … and it was so much fun that we’re doing it all over again with a bunch more great guests. Put on your Rust-o-Vision glasses, Neil freaks … we’re going deep into the Shakey-verse one more time. 

All One Song :: Ira Kaplan on “Big Crime” 

In case you’re wondering, the name of the podcast comes from a choice bit of Neil banter that showed up at the start of the 1997 live album Year of the Horse. “They all sound the same!” Neil yells. “It’s all one song!” 

But is it really all one song??? As we got further and further into that first season, we were constantly reminded that Neil’s discography is vast and diverse, the work of a supernaturally restless imagination. If it’s all one song, it’s a big song, a weird song, a long song. It’s your song, it’sour song. 

Most of all, it’s a great song, one that seems to only get richer and more meaningful as the decades go by. And it’s a song that’s still being written. Neil, of course, turned 80 years old last year, but it’s always inspiring to see how he finds his way into the current moment, always curious, always unwilling to fade away.

Which brings us to the first song of season two! Our guest today selected what is—as of this recording in March 2026—the newest Neil Young song that has made it out into the world: “Big Crime.” This angry, brutal and unsparing attack on ICE, Trump and the MAGA-sphere was debuted last summer on the US leg of Neil’s tour with the Chrome Hearts. And he’s played it at every one of his shows since. 

“Big Crime” pulls no punches. A sampling of the lyrics: 

Don’t need no fascist rules

Don’t want no fascist schools

Don’t want soldiers on our streets

There’s big crime in DC at the White House

Got to get the fascists out

got to clean the White House out

Don’t want soldiers on our streets

There’s big crime in DC at the White House

It might not be poetry exactly, but it gets the job done, right? Yo La Tengo certainly thought so. Last year, the long-running trio kicked off their epic eight-night Hanukkah run at the Bowery Ballroom in New York City with a blistering version of “Big Crime,” likely becoming the first band to cover it. So of course, we’re honored to have Yo La Tengo guitarist and vocalist Ira Kaplan on All One Song today to talk about this new Neil song. 

Yo La Tengo — do they need an introduction? In our opinion, they are simply the best American rock band of the last four decades. Ira Kaplan, Georgia Hubley and James McNew have built up a consistently wonderful body of work. Like Neil, it’s a career filled with twists and turns, side quests and sideways moves. But it’s all purely Yo La Tengo, whether they’re breaking your heart with hushed ballads or stretching out on feedback-fueled jams. Their latest album, 2023’s This Stupid World, is yet another masterpiece. And of course, they have a long history with Neil Young … the b-side of their second single was a sweet cover of “For The Turnstiles.” 

Suffice it to say, it was a total pleasure to talk “Big Crime” and further Neil topics with Ira Kaplan. Drop in and learn more.
