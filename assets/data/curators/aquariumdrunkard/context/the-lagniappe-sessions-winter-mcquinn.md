## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Fittingly, I happened upon Winter McQuinn and his associated Melbourne scene right around the time I was visiting Australia in 2023 and have kept rapt attention since. McQuinn’s third album Where Are We Now? is set to drop later this month, via the Sydney based Third Eye Stimuli Records, and with it his first Lagniappe Session. Here, McQuinn works up a full band arrangement of the autumnal 2017 Anna St. Louis chestnut “Fire,” before digging into Charles Brown’s “On The Corner” and Roger Miller’s 1973 adventure in animation courtesy of Robin Hood’s “Oo-De-Lally.” Yippee ki yay, indeed! McQuinn’s notes on his cover selections, below.

Winter McQuinn :: Fire (Anna St. Louis)

I remember coming across Anna St. Louis in 2021, deep lockdown mode and was watching a kind of bootleg compilation video that John Andrews had posted which included footage of Michael Hurley (RIP) and Anna St. Louis playing at some sort of outdoor gig. I remember being so drawn to the song and the calming balm that it evoked in me. Been on high rotation for me ever since and was really fun to work out a full band arrangement for it. 

Winter McQuinn :: On The Corner (Charles Brown)

Jack from Hot Apple Band introduced me to Charles Brown via “I Just Want To Talk To You” – a very great and awesome song and this one was next in line. Super beautiful harmony work and, again, was feeling inspired to work out a full band version of it. Big thanks to Pat for the 12 string lend too. 

Winter McQuinn :: Oo-De-Lally (Roger Miller)

I’d just gotten back from a UK tour where I’d had a day off and went to Sherwood Forest near Nottingham (!!). I guess I was feeling nostalgic for the classic Robin Hood animated series when I first heard this song. I knew that Murray would nail this version with me so kind of strong armed him into doing it with me one afternoon all on tape, a challenge but a pretty good outcome! The tape machine shortly broke after we finished this. Shoutout to Roger Miller, he lived a life! Was a fireman for a day, songwriter extraordinaire and soundtracked that iconic chicken.
