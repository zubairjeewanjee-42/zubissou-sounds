## Body

Out of all the genres, post-punk has always felt like the hardest to pin down. What the hell was / is it? I’m not sure I could tell you. But I can tell you that the Musicophilia blog’s massive 1981 box set of mixes is perhaps the best representation of the wild burst of creativity that was happening in the underground at the time. It’s a kaleidoscope of strange and dazzling sounds, all put together with a connoisseur’s ear and a fan’s heart. 1981 compiler Ian Manire writes: “If this set has a thesis, it’s that post-punk was defined not by its ‘greats,’ but by how great even its peripheral players were, as a movement that empowered people to both take over the means of production (and distribution and criticism and celebration, in ways that epitomized the ethos of ‘independent music’ to this day), and also the means of expression. It democratized artiness and attitude. You didn’t have to be spiky and energetic or arty and erudite: why not be both? And you didn’t need anyone’s permission.”

Originally a lovely handmade physical item that first emerged via message boards back in the mid-2000s, 1981 has since joined a host of other expertly curated Musicophilia mixes in the online realm. However you hear it, you’re in for a treat. Go ahead and dial into another frequency. | t wilcox
