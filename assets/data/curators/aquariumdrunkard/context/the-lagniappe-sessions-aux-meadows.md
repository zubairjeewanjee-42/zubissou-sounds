## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Aux Meadows, the Oakland-based trio of Steve Dawson (dobro, lap steel), Joe Imwalle (synths, piano) and Chris Royalty (guitar, bass), touch down for this latest installment of the Lagniappe Sessions. As noted in our Midyear Review, the group’s latest LP, Draw Near, is one of our favorite records of 2025, a sentiment that is only reaffirmed by the following three covers. Here, the trio works up Another Green World era Eno, Sonic Youth’s mid-90s high watermark “The Diamond Sea,” and “the killer” himself — Jerry Lee Lewis. Aux Meadows on their selections, below. 

Aux Meadows :: Golden Hours (Brian Eno)

We’re an instrumental group that mixes songs, improvisations, and ambient compositions—so it’s no surprise that Brian Eno is a major inspiration. In particular, Another Green World is a frequent reference, with its balance of evocative melodies, otherworldly textures, and moody interstitials.

We covered “Golden Hours” at the very first Aux Meadows performance (where we played to passersby from an empty parking space) and it’s been a live staple ever since. At our shows, we usually sing a couple surprise verses a capella at the end—but here, we’re mercifully offering just a taste. 

Aux Meadows :: The Diamond Sea (Sonic Youth)

Our humble tribute to an iconic epic. We wanted to emphasize the pensive vocal melody and add some rustic touches, while keeping many of the song’s signature sonic characteristics (skronky climax, sprawling outro, thick phaser). 

Aux Meadows :: Another Place, Another Time (Jerry Lee Lewis) 

Our favorite country songs are ones where the narrator’s personal struggles with life or love are so all-encompassing that they take on an almost cosmic scale. That’s certainly true when Jerry sings “Another Place, Another Time”. Is he waiting to see his beloved next week at another bar, or a hundred years from now on another planet? In this version, the lament builds with each verse, the string instruments falling away as the extradimensional synth comes in to whisk Jerry (and the rest of us) away into outer space.
