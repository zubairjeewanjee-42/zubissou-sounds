## Body

An introduction to Japanese folk in 1970s Japan, the following mix was created as a companion to the article on Modern Bible and the story of Gekidan Buraiha. It brings together a selection of early Japanese folk, protest songs and Angura recordings from the same era, providing additional context and atmosphere for the world the little theatre movement troupe emerged from.

First & Last: Angura (A Mixtape)

Wataru Takada – 自衛隊に入ろう = Join the Self-Defense Forces (1969): This is based on “Andorra,” an anti-war song released by Pete Seeger with lyrics containing paradoxical irony satirizing the Japanese Self-Defense Forces. However, when someone took the lyrics literally and actually enlisted in the Self-Defense Forces, Takada decided to retire the song.

Kan Mikami – 夢は夜ひらく = My Dreams Bloom at Night (1970): Kan Mikami started out his career on Columbia Records where he struggled because his songs kept getting banned from broadcasting. Kan Mikami later performed with Shuji Terayama’s theatre troupe Tenjo Sajiki.

J. A. Seazer – すべての人が死んで行く時に = When Everybody’s Going To Die (1970): The first recording by J. A. Seazer, released in 1970 when he joined Tenjo Sajiki.

Juro Kara – さすらいの唄 = The Wanderer’s Song (1970): Debut single by avant-garde playwright and theatre director Juro Kara released on King Records. Music and backing band by Hitoshi Komuro and his group Rokumonsen, of URC fame.

Tokyo Kid Brothers – アメリカの夢 = The American Dream (1971): Live recording of the theatre troupe Tokyo Kid Brothers, which may have been recorded at Café La MaMa in New York, where they performed in 1970. They later established a La MaMa in Tokyo, which does not appear to have had any official connection with Ellen Stewart’s Off-Off-Broadway venue other than the name.

J. A. Seazer & Tenjo Sajiki – 和讃 = Hymn (1973): Probably needs no introduction, one of the most known and beloved psychedelic records to ever come out of Japan.

Gekidan Buraiha – 泳ぎきれない重い雨 = A Heavy Rain That You Cannot Swim Through (1975): The most obscure and unknown of the angura records. Sung by actor Toru Okamoto. The song’s lyrics convey a sense of disillusionment with political activism, disappointment with student protests, resistance to becoming a cog in the machine of consumerism, and an overall sense of powerlessness shared among the aforementioned youths in the mid-1970s.

Kyokubakan – 旅の役者で御座居ます = I Am A Traveling Performer (1978): Another relatively unknown angura troupe. This self-released record is rumored to have had most of its copies destroyed in a fire.

Juro Kara & Jokyo Gekidan – ジョンシルバーの合唱 = John Silver’s Chorus (1977): Live recording from 1973 released on Bellwood Records circa 1977 from one of Juro Kara’s most famous plays, John Silver: The Beggar of Love.

Yosuke Yamashita Trio & Dairakudakan – ゴースト パートII = Ghosts Part II( 1977): A bit of a bonus track. Buraiha’s lead actor, Mr. Takahashi, became a Butoh dancer shortly after leaving the troupe. This underrated record by the Yosuke Yamashita Trio features Dairakudakan, one of the first Butoh dance troupes, founded in 1972. I haven’t asked Mr. Takahashi if they were an inspiration, but I would not be surprised to hear that they were.
