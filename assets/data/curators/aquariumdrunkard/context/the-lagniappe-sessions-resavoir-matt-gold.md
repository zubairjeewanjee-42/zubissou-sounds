## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Trumpeter/composer/producer Will Miller (AKA Resavoir) and guitarist Matt Gold released the collab LP Horizon almost exactly a year ago. The album was an expertly rendered love letter to the música popular brasileira world, its 10 tracks breezy, beautiful and deep. Horizon is lush without feeling overdone, intricate but never fussy, favoring groove and melody above all else. These two musicians may call Chicago home, but the sounds they make transport the listener directly to a Brazilian beach. Now, Miller and Gold are offering up a pair of bonus tracks for their debut Lagniappe Session together — gorgeous covers of MPB icons Mario Adnet and Caetano Veloso. The duo’s notes on their selections below …

Resavoir :: Ela (Mario Adnet)

“Ela” is a tune I first heard on Madlib Medicine Show No. 2: Flight to Brazil. I got into this album in my early 20s, fresh out of college and living in my first apartment in Chicago. There wasn’t as much access to rare grooves and discovery algorithms as there is now, so it required a certain amount of digging and word of mouth to find out about lesser known artists from around the world. Luckily we had Madlib to put us onto the dope shit. Revisiting this mix now I’m realizing this track is a part of track 3, “Belo Horizonte”, which was also an early working title for the album Matt and I ultimately decided to title “Horizon”.

In addition to Madlib, someone who I really admire as a discoverer and sharer of great and lesser known vinyl records is Mejiwahn, who included Joyce’s version of “Ela” on one of his mixes I was rinsing while on vacation last winter. Upon hearing it for the first time in a long time it instantly felt so familiar and I became obsessed with the song all over again, learning how to play it on piano and thinking it would be a fun song to cover. I just love the harmonic movement and nuances of this song so much, the melodic variations, the arrangement… it’s a special tune and was super fun to put this stripped back version together with Matt. 

Resavoir :: Lua (Caetano Veloso)

Caetano’s music has been a constant inspiration to me ever since I first encountered it, probably when I was about 17 or 18 years old. I heard Caetano Veloso (1969) first, falling in love with the songs, the production, the psychedelia intermingling with the folkloric traditions. But I love music from many periods of his catalogue, all the way up to the present day — so much variation and yet always rooted, sounding like himself at every turn.

“Lua” comes from 1975’s Jóia, and seemed like a world that Will and I could step into and make our own. There’s so much space in the melody, the pulse, the arrangement; when I’m thinking about covering someone’s music, I’m always looking for that sense of space, an opening that might hold other voices. Where the original groove inhabits an understated, meditative space, I heard the possibility of setting it in a more electronic realm, surrounding the song’s perfectly hypnotic melody with a busier texture of contrasting elements. The melody envelops you, winding around the pulse with the slightest variation at each turn. Our version builds and branches off from that initial hypnotic, understated space, arriving at something a little darker, a little later-at-night.
