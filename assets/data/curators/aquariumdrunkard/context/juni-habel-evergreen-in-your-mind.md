## Body

Singing of nature, creation, and ghosts, the Norwegian singer-songwriter Juni Habel has crafted an arresting collection of skeletal and gently psychedelic folk on her third album, Evergreen In Your Mind. Her voice, a trembling quiver, is ghostly against understated instrumentation — often not much more than plaintive guitar, trickling piano, and quietly askew atmospherics. Incidental sounds and room tone take residence in the songs, still and subtle worlds that are sweetly delicate and hauntingly adrift.

There’s a feverish hush to Habel’s music, where restless tempests of desire and longing unfold in settings of solitary quietude. There’s a wild restraint to her approach — a storm in the distance that, while not necessarily heard, is deeply and wholly felt. Evoking a pastoral melancholy or a seaside escapism, the music assumes an impressionistic pose, with sighing pedal steel, muted percussion, and gossamer hums on the hypnotic instrumental “Pearl Cloud Song” and Habel’s voice emanating like a chilled breath in the wintry dark on the meditative title track, the tune flickering like a desperately kept fire against an unrelenting cold.

Evoking the stark nature of Sybille Baier or the mysteriously chamberesque qualities of Department of Eagles, Evergreen In Your Mind is a stunning and timeless work. Vulnerable yet self-possessed, Habel has created something that feels like a hushed secret which, once unlocked, reveals a magnitude of beauty too vivid and rare to be tethered to the earth. | c depasquale
