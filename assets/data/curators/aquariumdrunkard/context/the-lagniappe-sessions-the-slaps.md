## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Via Chicago, guitarist Rand Kelly, bassist Ramsey Bell, and drummer Josh Resing are The Slaps. Formed in 2017, the genre agnostic trio are constantly in motion, and, more impressively, consistently interesting (do check out their most recent 2025 single “Wettest Wing” for a taste). This installment of the Lagniappe Sessions finds the band paying tribute to pre-Dark Side era Floyd with a lived-in, and woolly, cover of Obscured by Clouds‘ “Wot’s… Uh the Deal”. AD Manna, indeed! And keeping things local, they also take on hemlock’s “Under All The Kudzu” originally found on the 2023 November collection. The Slaps in their own words, below. 

The Slaps :: Under All The Kudzu (Hemlock)

Covering these two songs was a total joy! We workshopped our versions live on the road while touring through the summer and fall then hopped in Jamdek Chicago and tracked ’em live. “Under all that Kudzu” is written by Carolina Chauffee, aka hemlock, a close friend and touring partner who wrote this song a year ago. Rand really dug it and presented this heavier version, we leaned into an ’80’s Athens, GA sound after picking up an REM DVD in Glasgow. Fitting for a song about kudzu. 

The Slaps :: Wot’s… Uh the Deal (Pink Floyd)

The Slaps’s favorite band is Pink Floyd. Floyd’s music offers a lot of advice and foretells the trials of being a band in business. Obscured by Clouds is Pink Floyd’s 1972 record where they were exploring much of the studio techniques that would come to full effect on Dark Side. “Wot’s… Uh the Deal” is a song that spans the band’s early career and chronicles their feelings as they approached mainstream success. The Slaps are closing in on 10 years as a band, marking the end of their first era.

Songs engineered by Doug Malone, Jamdek. Songs mastered by Mike Nolte, Eureka Mastering
