## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Tracked in New Orleans at Bruisey Peets’ Lake Vista compound on a Tascam 388, for their debut Lagniappe Session Silver Synthetic add their steady brand of choogle to Coney Island Baby era Lou Reed, some late ’70s Chris Spedding and, naturally, give a nod to the mount Rushmore of the genre via JJ Cale’s “Wish I Had Not Said That.” Hit with a near city wide black out while recording, the band packed their car full of gear and went looking for electricity. The lights were on at Funky Nola LLC, where they finished out the tunes. Silver Synthetic in their own words, below.

Silver Synthetic :: Charley’s Girl (Lou Reed)

Coney Island Baby was in heavy rotation around the time the band formed, so it felt only natural to dust this one off. Lou really does it. Don’t bore us, get to the chorus. I think we’ve all known a Charley’s girl at one time or another.

Silver Synthetic :: Video Life (Chris Spedding)

Another song that was circulating a lot in the early days of Silver Synthetic. We talked about covering it over the years and finally got around to it. This song rips. It has a kind of bizarre arrangement/form that sounds like they were just throwing shit down to tape to see what would stick, yet it all came together in a pretty cohesive and deliberate way. The results are for sure undeniable. Hats off to Chris Spedding, a real rocker.

Silver Synthetic :: Wish I Had Not Said That (JJ Cale)

JJ Cale was living in a trailer park in Anaheim in 1980 when recording Shades. Working between Nashville and California. That image really lines up with the mood of this song for me. Distant, regretful, bittersweet. A low-down feeling that comes with a reward.

Credits: Background Vocals (Tracks 1,2): Casey Jane Reece-Kaigler. Recorded by Bruisey Peets and Kunal Prakash. Mixed by Ross Farbe. Mastered by Timothy Stollenwerk at Stereophonic Mastering.
