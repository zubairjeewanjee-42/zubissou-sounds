## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Last fall saw the release of the Sydney, Australia based DIY artist Chet Sounds’ sophomore LP, Changes Happen to Everyone, Everywhere. At a dozen tracks, it’s a lo-fi glossy and groove-laden trip across 70s-am pop, yacht rock, private press outsider folk, library funk, and Rundgren-esque psychedelia. For this installment of the Lagniappe Sessions we catch up with Chet (Tucker) as he works his way through a grip of disparate favorites, ranging from a mid-60s fantasy sit-com theme, to reinterpreting Judee Sill and the undisputed majesty that is Procol Harum’s “A Whiter Shade of Pale”. The artist in his own words, below. 

Chet Sounds :: Bewitched Theme (Howard Greenfield and Jack Keller)

This theme song has been stuck in my head ever since I first started watching the TV show as a little child with my mother. I always imagined a version with guitar and synths. I tried to make the lead synthesiser melody imitate whistling.

Chet Sounds :: Down Where The Valleys Are Low (Judee Sill)

I haven’t heard any covers of this song, so I thought I would try to do my own stripped-back version. It would have been impossible to recreate Judee’s version when it was already perfect, so I just went for a lo-fi folky angle. Louie Shelton, who played on the original recording, is one of my favourite guitarists.

Chet Sounds :: A Whiter Shade of Pale (Procol Harum)

This is one of my favourite songs ever, and I know it has been covered quite a bit, but I still wanted to give it a go. I didn’t play the same lead melody as the original but improvised a lead guitar, hinting at similar melodic phrases. I was trying to imagine what it would sound like if Robbie Robertson played guitar on the track.
