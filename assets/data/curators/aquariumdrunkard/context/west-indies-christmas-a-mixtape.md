## Body

The holidays, West Indies style. With the expansion of record distribution from the UK, the US, and Canada during the 1960s, Christmas music experienced a notable surge, mirroring developments in Jamaica. The following twenty track all vinyl mixtape covers most of that time period, from early the 60s through the late 70s.

Jamaica was quick to catch on. Figures like Byron Lee became connectors, scouting talent from Barbados and Trinidad & Tobago and bringing them to Kingston to record and collaborate. The Opels were one such discovery. Kalyan even found themselves opening for The Commodores and the Jackson 5, while Boo & The Tru-Tones landed a surprise hit with their cover of Hot Chocolate’s “You Sexy Thing,” later taking the stage at the Super Bowl XIII halftime show in 1979 for a Carnival-themed performance. Wild Fire—originally known as The Sparks—rounded out the scene. Many of the labels behind this music—WIRL, Trex—operated out of Kingston, even as their catalogs leaned heavily toward Barbados and Trinidad & Tobago. | Ryan Baxley
