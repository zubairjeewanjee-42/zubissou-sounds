## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Earlier this summer the Geneva, Switzerland based L’Eclair released their fourth LP, Cloud Drifter, via our neighborhood friends down the hill at Innovative Leisure. We’ve been following the Swiss outfit since Frank Maston turned us onto them in 2019 when the group supported his stateside tour, and later recorded the 2021 collaborative album, Souvenir. For their debut Lagniappe Session, L’Eclair reimagines some 1979 disco heat via Anita Ward’s “Ring My Bell,” embrace the street soul of Lisa Baron’s 1990 “Lovin N Affection,” and engage with something more recent in the form of Beach House’s now decade-old “Space Song.” The group on their selections, below . . .

L’Eclair :: Ring My Bell (Anita Ward)

An old-time banger we wanted to rework with a bouncier groove inspired by early 2000s productions. We’re also huge fans of the dub cover from the Blood Sisters which can set on fire every dance floor in the galaxy. The track was also perfect for our first collaboration with Erin Kimberly, one of the most promising singer coming out of the Swiss Groove scene. Watch out for her Space Travelers.

L’Eclair :: Space Song (Beach House)

We discovered Kay Yoko’s music around the same time as We were working on the covers for AD & we decided to reach out for some funk action. She was the one who proposed “Space Song” and for some reason we never really got to be huge Beach House fans so we were pretty excited to work on a « Megadef version » (l’Eclair’s touch) of this modern classic. Also, listen to Kay Yoko please, she has some bangers.

L’Eclair :: Lovin N Affection (Lisa Baron)

Annnnnd that’s another banger. During COVID, we developed a cosmic obsession with Street Soul, a British music sub-genre blending early hip-hop with synths, heavy basses & soulful vocals which feels like proto-Beyoncé stuff. This song has been on repeat while also not being available on the streaming services. We had to cover it and We called our favorite funk partner Inès Mouzoune who sings on our song “Cloud Drifter” & shreds in her band Roshâni. PLAY IT LOUD.
