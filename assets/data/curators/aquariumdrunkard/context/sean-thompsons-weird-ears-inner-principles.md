## Body

Sean Thompson seems to be leading several different musical lives at once. The Nashville-based guitarist is a trusty hired gun, lending his dazzling six-string skills to such Music City mainstays as Margo Price and Erin Rae; he’s one-third of the awesome kosmische trio Shrunken Elvis alongside Rich Ruth and Spencer Cullum — their self-titled 2025 debut was an absolute stunner; he’s also got a handful of killer Weird Ears records that are filled with pleasingly eccentric country-rock and tasty licks.

The latest Weird Ears LP, Inner Principles, shows us yet another side of Thompson’s restless imagination. Entirely instrumental, it features ECM-dosed visions a la John Abercrombie, smoky acoustic picking, laid-back boogies, and sleek post rock-y adventures. Throughout, we get to enjoy elemental interplay between the guitarist and a fine selection of Nashville ringers, including the aforementioned Rich Ruth, drummer Eric Slick, soundscaper Jake Davis, bassist Alec Newman, and keyboardist Jo Schornikow. The album is currently a digital only situation, but that will be remedied by Western Vinyl who will be pressing it up on wax in the very near future.

Inner Principles might have listeners playing a game of spot-the-influence at times, but Thompson is more than just a guitarist with great taste. The eight absorbing tracks here are deeply felt, finding emotional resonance and richness in virtually every moment. To borrow from an Abercrombie classic, this one’s timeless. | t wilcox
