## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

When we last checked in on Sheffield’s Bobby Lee circa 2023’s Endless Skyways, the songwriter had “finally reached the horizon he’d been headed toward, only to take a flying leap beyond it.” Proudly flying the fried/spaced-out/cosmic country banner from the UK, Lee’s tunes ramble and barrel forward—even when they sink into languid stoned grooves. Not long ago, a a treat from Bobby recently landed in our inbox: a three song set of covers from artist like Don Williams, Fred Eaglesmith, and Cymande. Featuring Lee’s rare vocals, these songs showcase his deep head taste and penchant for exceptional grooves. Strap in and rocket toward the stars—Bobby Lee is at the controls.

Bobby Lee :: Tulsa Time (Don Williams)

Crawling out from underneath my rock with a couple of non-instrumental songs. I’d been thinking that it was a shame that neither JJ Cale nor Marc Bolan ever recorded “Tulsa Time,” so I’m trying to walk that line. Velvet-n-stars-elfen-glam-rock-chug and dusty-rhythm-box-powered-DIY-laidback-country-groovers aren’t such unlikely bedfellows. That’s my boy Joe Harvey-Whyte on pedal steel here, doing his best Sneaky Pete. 

Bobby Lee :: Trucker Speed (Fred Eaglesmith)

Military-grade blue collar existentialism with a pill-popping chorus that’s the best kind of poetry. Fred Eaglesmith’s sparse and ragged original is perfect but I wanted to give it some of that woozy, crusty, languid feeling you get when you’ve been traveling too far for too long. Cramped seat, trying to sleep to pass the time, occasional flashes of sunlight blinding you, with a fourth generation C90 (which may have been left to bake on the dashboard) on endless repeat. 

Bobby Lee :: One More (Cymande)

I love Cymande’s use of space and Patrick Patterson’s guitar playing is killer. It was “Dove” that got me hooked on the band years ago but I’ve been turning to “One More” recently. We play a lot of spaced-out funk and fuzzy library stuff in the van and I guess I’m channelling some of that with this version. Eagle-eared listeners might be able to spot the drum break.
