## Body

Masayoshi Takanaka’s All of Me, originally issued in 1979, gathers material from the guitarist’s early solo run into a remarkably fluid sequence. Reissued for 2026 Japan Record Store Day and newly remastered at Abbey Road, the collection still moves with startling ease: polished AOR, tropical fusion, bossa drift, and instrumental pop unfolding in long, sunlit lines.

Takanaka was still in his twenties when All of Me appeared, already recording at a relentless pace after time spent with Sadistic Mika Band and Flied Egg. The music collected here captures him in constant motion. “Oh! Tengo Suerte,” “Malibu,” and “Ready To Fly” glide on elastic basslines, layered percussion, soft-focus synthesizers, and Takanaka’s unmistakable guitar tone — bright, agile, and seemingly weightless. Even at full velocity, the arrangements retain an unusual looseness, as though the songs are surfing their own momentum.

What lingers is the atmosphere surrounding the playing. Takanaka rarely treats virtuosity as the main event. Rhythm guitar, backing vocals, strings, and percussion remain central to the music’s architecture, giving the record a warmth and openness that still feels distinctive nearly fifty years later. All of Me belongs to a particular late-70s Pacific imagination: coastal highways, salt air, mirrored sunlight, FM radio after dark. Music built for movement and horizon lines. | m wright
