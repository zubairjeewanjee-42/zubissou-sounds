## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

In the midst of a northeast tour Sam Moss stopped in to Pale Moon Services in Cambridge, NY for an afternoon where he spent some time meeting, working with, and being guided by studio proprietor Jared Samuel and assistant engineer Victor Pacek. The day culminated in the following four covers, including Townes Van Zandt’s anthropomorphizing a window, 1979 Leonard Cohen, a Willie Nelson chestnut, and Jenn Wasner’s Flock of Dimes. Moss on his selections, below. 

Sam Moss :: The Traitor (Leonard Cohen)

I first heard this song sung by Martha Wainwright in the concert film I’m Your Man, which was really my proper introduction to Leonard Cohen’s work. I still remember watching it in the theater, at sixteen, being totally swept up in the mercurialness of this song. Years later, I still haven’t quite grasped it. I like what Cohen had to say about “The Traitor” in that film: “It was about the feeling that we have of betraying some mission that we were mandated to fulfill, and being unable to fulfill it. And then coming to understand that the real mandate was not to fulfill it and that the deeper courage was to stand guiltless in the predicament in which you found yourself.”

Sam Moss :: One More Hour (Flock of Dimes)

I found so many songs to carry with me from Flock of Dimes’ 2021 album, Head of Roses. Through her work in Wye Oak and as Flock of Dimes, I have long admired Jenn Wasner’s songwriting and have always been drawn to Wye Oak’s ability to take a deeply intimate song and surround it with huge, dense sound. I wish I knew how to do this. Head of Roses puts the lyrics a little more at the center than some of those heavier Wye Oak albums, and it is one of my favorite albums of recent years. This one feels like a universal anthem for the passage of time. That line “If I could have anything I’d take one more hour” will be kicking around my head for years.

Sam Moss :: Be Here To Love Me (Townes Van Zandt)

This is probably my favorite TVZ song. He anthropomorphizes a window! Also, “The poets are demanding their pay/And they’ve left me with nothing to say” is one of the best lines about writing. In his long list of love songs, I don’t think there is any that feels more real and true to me. Plus the flute on the original recording is perfect. If someone asks me to sing a love song, this is the one I always think of first.

Sam Moss :: Stay Away From Lonely Places (Willie Nelson)

As someone who usually writes short songs without too many parts, I look to Willie Nelson as a guiding force for how to do it. I return to his songs daily to sit with their economy and depth. I don’t usually go for advice songs, but I love this one for its impossible demand.

Session Notes: Aside from guitar and singing, I play a bit of violin on “Stay Away From Lonely Places”. Victor plays bass on a couple tunes, and Jared is responsible for all the other lovely and strange textures.
