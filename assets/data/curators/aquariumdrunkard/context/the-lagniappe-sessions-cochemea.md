Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Cochemea Gastelum returns with his second Lagniappe entry following up his session from 2021 covering Big Star and the legendary Cuban band, Irakere. This latest installment finds the NYC based multi-instrumentalist and arranger on the heels of his third LP, Ancestros Futuros, released last month via Daptone Records. For his encore session, Gastelum pays tribute to his southern California roots covering War’s “All Day Music,” and Flip Your Wig era Hüsker Dü. Cochemea’s notes on his selects, below.

Cochemea :: All Day Music (War)

War’s “All Day Music” was the first record I ever bought by one of my favorite bands. Being from Southern California, their music was part of growing up. This song has always evoked a soothing quality for me — an invitation to gather, to be together, to let the day unfold through playing, loving, and simply being. I approached it more like a ballad, keeping the arrangement open and warm, with a slowed-down beat on the Maestro Rhythm King, vibed-out Fender Rhodes, and electric sax.

Cochemea :: Keep Hanging On (Hüsker Dü)

Hüsker Dü’s music has a raw emotional charge that I’ve felt deeply since I was a youngster going to shows. I’ve always loved Grant Hart’s vocals on “Keep Hanging On” — the way he sings like he could be calling out to a lover or a friend, with an urgency in his screams that opens the heart. I imagined a version of the song more like a plea or a prayer of ecstatic devotion. I was listening to “Love Will Find a Way” from Harvest Time by Pharoah Sanders while working on it — the opening conga part from that track found its way into this one, and the overall sound of that album influenced my approach.

Both of these songs carry a kind of spirit in them. They’re about holding on — to community, to memory, to love, to life itself.

Credits: All instruments performed by Cochemea, except bongos on “All Day Music” by Elizabeth Pupo-Walker. Recorded to Tascam 388 in his home studio.
