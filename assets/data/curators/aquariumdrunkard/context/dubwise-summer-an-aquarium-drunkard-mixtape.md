## Body

Summer equals dub. It is the elemental music of the season. The tropical island birthplace of dub boasts an average annual temperature in the low eighties; and the genre’s pioneers managed to infuse that pervasive mugginess into every inch of the sound. Everything in dub feels suspended in the high humidity: a little slower than usual; a little hazier—delayed in the atmosphere. The rattlesnake bass, snatches of passerby vocals, smears of horn, the mirage of melodica, the easy guitar skank rising and falling like a welcome breeze off the water, and those beats battering down like the sun itself. All of it slightly warped and shimmering in the noonday heat. Dub belongs to the warm weather. Perhaps this is why those of us who aren’t in Jamaica find ourselves reaching for it during the summer months. There is something about the languor and low stakes of dub that feels especially appropriate this time of year. It can punch without getting too aggro; it can bewitch without getting too psychedelic. It is open-ended, but not without structure and logic. As head music goes, it is toasted, rather than hallucinogenic. Dub makes us move, but too fast, and not too much. That beautifully blunted, medium-tempo momentum might be all anyone needs in this weather. In that spirit, here are a couple hours of some choice riddims from the golden age of dub. Stay positive, stay hydrated and enjoy. | b sirota
