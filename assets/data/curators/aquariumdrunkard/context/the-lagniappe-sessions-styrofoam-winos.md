## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Hailing from Music City, USA, Styrofoam Winos are Lou Turner, Joe Kenkel and Trevor Nikrant. Following up one of our favorite albums of 2024, the Winos return this month with their debut Lagniappe Session. With four covers as eclectic and malleable as their collective influences, the trio lean into the Roches’ 1979 s/t cult classic, the wonder that is “Blue” Gene Tyranny, peak and primal Exene Cervenka, and a cut via Link Wray’s inestimable 3-Track Shack era. Press play and dig into the band’s notes on their selections, below.

Styrofoam Winos :: Next Time Might Be Your Time (“Blue” Gene Tyranny)

We all found “Blue” Gene Tyranny around the time Out of the Blue was reissued by Unseen Worlds in 2019. What a ride! This song though, in particular, is a timeless gem. The melody is unreal—a pop tune, for sure, but with strange turns that feel somehow inevitable. We’ve been playing the song on tour a lot this year, trading off verses and joining in together for the choruses. We’re suckers for songs about time and songs about friendship. How bout a mystical number about both? Dear companion… Why don’t you take a break from whatever puts the pressure on you? / Oh, listen, can you go back to before you were born?…We’re not attached or separate in space. Can’t do it better. Ours is a stripped-down version. It doesn’t have the facemelting guitar solo or the horn section outro—you’ll have to go to  the original for that. Trust in Rock! – JK

Styrofoam Winos :: Quitting Time (The Roches)

The Roches (1979) is a masterpiece that we’ve all loved for the past several years. “Quitting Time” is definitely up there with their best and is also just a favorite song of mine, period. It’s an all-timer, one of the greats about new beginnings. Thought doing a countrified version might suit the melody and let the song shine kind of unadorned. Our harmonies are much simpler than the Roches’, but they felt amazing to sing in our own Wino-fashion. Pedal steel (Trev) and drums (LT) fit the tune like a glove. – JK

Styrofoam Winos :: The World’s A Mess, It’s in My Kiss (X)

I love how X bring country elements into their punk songs, with straightforward poetry and strange harmonies. This one is a long-time go-to when feeling overwhelmed. It’s an apocalyptic rallying cry, but also super playful. We were curious about how it would feel to slow it down while keeping the groove and intensity. Felt good! Trev ripped the steel and Joe played his signature Crumar Roadrunner (which you can also hear on the BGT cover and on Real Time). – LT

Styrofoam Winos :: Tucson, Arizona (Link Wray)

I love the sort of chilled-out existential side of Link Wray that comes out on some of his 70s albums, and this one is an all-timer. His 3-Track Shack albums are some of my favorite recordings, but it’s also cool to hear the Thomas Jefferson Kaye production on this one (Be What You Want To [1973]). It’s got a real classic country feel and we wanted to follow that in our arrangement because it’s just too fun not too. Shook it up a little though with Joe riding the Korg MS-20 filter sweeps on the bridge. It’s also just really fun to sing this melody and I would recommend trying it. – TN
