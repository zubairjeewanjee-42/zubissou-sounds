## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Nearly a decade into their sonic trans-dimensional sojourn, Wet Tuna’s Vast, slingshots us sidelong toward the very heart of the Tunaverse— a place where the bass is deep and the vibes flow free down that shimmering stretch of good ol’ astral highway. With this installment of the Lagniappe Sessions, Matt Valentine, Erika Elder, and Jim Bliss serve up a dubbed out Spectrasound love letter to the glorious fuzz’n’scuzz of yesterday’s underground. The Tuna guide us on a rural glam walking tour of downtown NYC with an ever-unfolding take on Lou Reed’s “Walk On the Wild Side,” an exploration of “Cortez the Killer” that detours into Daydream-era Sonic Youth, nods to venerable free folk progenitors Pearls Before Swine and Sandy Bull, and a glacially resplendent meditation on The Jesus and Mary Chain’s “Just Like Honey” that’s basically psychoactive. Many wonders abound here, so get in there and touch the sound! Matt Valentine in his own words, below.

We’ve always had a small is beautiful philosophy, the child of Microtones releases were (still are!) mostly editions of 99 because they weren’t really intended to reach an audience beyond that. the concept is akin to having a chance encounter stravaiging in the forest and discovering someone playing music to the trees. the dynamic, presentation and cerebral discourse that occurs with that kind of event I don’t think truly has an adjective yet. With Vast and the current singularity our brushstroke felt to have a broader canvas even tho it was more DIY than ever. That’s the conceptual art behind these choices.

Wet Tuna :: Walk on the Wild Side (Lou Reed)

Back in my “New York” days I met Lou once, I had been partying with my friends who had an apt on Bleecker Street, we were listening to Transformer and in the deep night with pretty loose mind I took a walk with my friend down to the Hudson via cobbled backstreets eventually to Christopher Street… this was 90’s NYC and was still slightly gnar at that hour especially in the dimly lit corners. We walked by a person sitting on a bench totally isolated and out comes “can I bum a cigarette”. I knew instantly that it was Lou Reed. so I gave him a smoke and we talked for a minute. That was a sign…another time years later when Erika & I were touring Portugal I had a similar experience in Lisbon. We had been working with a promoter there for years who was a colorful cat and I think we had 4 or 5 shows that he handled en route to Lisbon from the north. After the first one we went to settle up and the presenter said oh we already paid…?!?…we thought we were being scammed but verified the next day that ALL the Portuguese shows were settled in advance and we’d be getting the coin at the end of the run “for protection”. Ok…so after the big finale we had a ultra mondo bash Lisbon style and then somehow never went to bed. Deep deep night our promoter/host said ‘stay with me bail on yr lodging’ and ‘let’s take a drive I wanna show you some of the town in nocturne’. More dimly lit corners…we are in his whip, Yugo size, and “Walk On The Wild Side” comes on the radio. We went into some super deep wasteland zones, prostitution, heavy addiction, heavy usage, heavy scoring, heavy homeless…very different feel than the USA analog of same. Way more “surreal”. Anyway, we were fascinated and he pulls into an alley and says let’s get out…so we did and it’s pouring rain…and we go into a barely lit zonkoid feeling corridor. Cat takes off his shoe, asks me to hold it, takes off his sock (which is soaked) and out of it pulls out a wad of money. Says here’s the take from the tour starts cracking up and we drove home while “WOWS” was fading out.

Wet Tuna :: Cortez The Killer > Providence (Neil Young/Sonic Youth)

Another New York era flavor here. I started seeing Sonic Youth live at CB’s circa Evol and would catch them live downtown NYC often. I bought Daydream Nation the day it came out and when I heard “Providence” I thought it was the greatest move in the omniverse. I’d play it over and over, it fascinated me. much like I’d played Zuma over and over and over as a teen. I thought (and still do) that it is one of the greatest records of all time. “Cortez” has held a special place as a blown out jam monster and seems to stop time. When they came to Madison Square Garden together it blew my mind. When Wet Tuna starts playing any Neil tunes the energy becomes paramount, in our fantasy jam space we think of Neil’s “catalog” kinda like Garcia with Dylan’s. With Tuna we know intuitively if we’ve tapped into the cosmic storehouse or not. We did two takes when we were preparing for this session, the first take was a bit faster and didn’t quite get lost…we knew with the second take that this was the vibe we wanted to find off trail. Mick Flower has long been a seeker like ourselves and an enabler for us. We’ve played alotta music together and hopefully have more to find. It made perfect sense when he got in touch while I was finishing this mix to ask him to be Watt, the synchronicity was unreal so he left a message on the machine.

Wet Tuna :: Oh Dear (Miss Morse) (Pearls Before Swine)

I’m a huge ESP disk fan and a massive Tom Rapp enthusiast. We became friends in his later years thanks to things like Ptolemaic Terrascope/stock and he would call quite often back in the dial up days. That seems even more charming now. I love his songwriting style and in my old band The Tower Recordings I would often use the first two Pearls Before Swine albums as “templates” for the music I was reaching for. We would do a version of “I Saw The World” back then. Anyway, I’ve sat in with Damon & Naomi doing “Translucent Carraiges” and I flirted with some of the other ones but I felt “Miss Morse” really captured the mood of the times when I first started Tuna. In the beginning it was super primitive and it’s grown into a prog statement for us. We do alotta 3 part harmony singing on Vast and it directly came out of our efforts/arrangement of “Miss Morse”. It seems to capture the feeling of the times and current communication tech even more wildly now.

Wet Tuna :: Just Like Honey (The Jesus And Mary Chain)

I saw JAMC at their first ever New York show at a place called The World. The Pixies opened. Coincidentally I also saw Neil there with the Bluenotes. The Mary Chain came on really late and the place was rammed. Wasted vibe. It was glorious. I had already bought Psychocandy and was stoked that the noise was in mega raw three dimensionality live. The melody too…there was a longish minute (maybe it’s still going) where my daughter was digging 80’s music (The Cure/Siouxsie/Cocteaus…she really took to the Mary Chain esp. “Just Like Honey” so we listened to it alot in the house a year or so ago)…we just started jamming in a throbbing gristle basement noise style having fun with this slow groove at the end of a recent Tuna rehearsal and this came out.

Wet Tuna :: Cheeseburger (Sandy Bull)

Sandy Bull’s music and approach means alot to me. He is free folk, opened alotta doors. It was incredible to meet him at the Cooler in NYC in the 90’s, maybe Tower opened. I can’t remember. Pretty sure his daughter played tambura. His approach had a huge impact on me wanting to play ethnic instruments and attempt collabos with people like Chris Corsano. The early limited editions on Our Child of Microtones label were conceived in a lets reach for the E Pluribus Unum style. This track “Cheeseburger” was always a fascinating and genius statement to me on his final 70s studio record. Maybe it garnered extra publishing revenue? Maybe it’s the ultimate avant-garde move? Maybe it was goodbye in analog…

I look at the versions on these recordings as a natural extension to the Wet Tuna spectrasound approach. This concept of songs that are familiar and intimate but can be informed by avant sensibilities, dub techniques and forays into improvisation…also searching and tapping into the songs longform energy to discover experimental flourishes that can be found between the lines of melody. We try to embrace the environments and harmonic tradition the song can lead you to with off trail elements of conceptual art, free folk and sonic architecture.

Session Notes: Matt Valentine: vocals/guitar/electric piano/synths/percussion/congas/drum programming. Erika Elder: synthesizers/vocals/space drums. Jim Bliss: bass/synthesizers/vocal/percussion. Special guest: Mick Flower long distance phone call (“Providence”). Recorded at Sweet Pond Studio, Vermont.
