## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

It’s easy to imagine Philly’s Jeffrey Alexander never leaving the recording studio. In addition to his work with The Heavy Lidders and Dire Wolves, he also releases spaced out, heady tunes under his own name. His latest Bandcamp drop is The Snailhook Tapes Vol 4, and to celebrate its release, he’s back with another Lagniappe Session, offering supremely stoned and drifting takes on classics by Harry Nilsson, Horace Silver and Salome Bey, The Smiths, and Mike Williams. Liquid drips of guitar, percussion, and Alexander’s craggy Neil Young-ish folk abound—wade into these murky waters for a refreshing feeling indeed.

Jeffrey Alexander :: Coconut (Harry Nilsson)

I’m a firm believer that all the best books have maps in them. And so it goes that all the best songs to cover have just one chord. This is clearly a giant airy C7 masterpiece—so much fun to jam on! And I don’t get a lot of opportunities to play my hooter—or my tambourine, for that matter. Thankfully Ryan Jewell provided all the other percussion!

Jeffrey Alexander :: Forever Is A Long Long Time (Horace Silver with Salome Bey)

One of my favorite tracks from Silver’s meditative United Stated Of Mind series (this is from the third one: 1972’s All). Such a spacious groove and contemplative lyrics—and also featuring the stunning contralto vocals from Canada’s First Lady of Blues. I wanted to go for a cosmik 4-track DIY version and took a stab at those jazzy 6/9 chords with heavy use of an analog phaser, which I thought worked well alongside my moog synthesizer.

Jeffrey Alexander :: These Things Take Time (The Smiths) 

As a teenager, I truly loved The Smiths—in 1984 my local record shop in Baltimore turned me on to an import of Hatful Of Hollow which I simply played to death. Like I literally had to buy another copy. It was so cool—the gatefold sleeve, the catchy tunes, the whole vibe. I had the pleasure of seeing them twice in concert on the Queen Is Dead tour in 86 and although Morrissey sadly turned out to be a huge bummer all-around, those first few records have stuck with me. Was always Marr anyway.

Jeffrey Alexander :: Smoke Out The Memory (Mike Williams)

Self-released his first album The Radio Show in 1977. And I’m a real sucker for 70s privately pressed LPs (I even just started a new private-press-only radio show for NQRA). Also, I found it in the dollar bin at the great Acme Records in Milwaukee (I’m even more of a sucker for dollar bin vinyl). So roll up a bomber! 

Credits: Jeffrey Alexander – vocals, electric + acoustic guitars, electric bass, piano, moog MG-1, organ, percussion, melodica, electronics, tapes, fake mellotron, maestro rhythm king with special guest Ryan Jewell – hand percussion on Coconut Ryan recorded his tracks at Speaking Mountain Studios in Highmount NY.
