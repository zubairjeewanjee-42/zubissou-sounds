## Body

Lagniappe (la ·gniappe) noun ‘lan-ˌyap,’ — 1. An extra or unexpected gift or benefit. 2. Something given or obtained as a gratuity or bonus.

Under the moniker Adeline Hotel, New York-based musician Dan Knishkowy has spent nearly the last decade releasing one fantastic album after another. A benchmark identity of the project is that no release ever repeats quite the same sonic foray, a deliberate approach taking creative inspiration from the likes of Jim O’Rourke and Arthur Russell, the musician revealed to AD last year. After hearing that sound mutate from fingerpicking guitar to the jazzy orchestral pop of Hot Fruit to last year’s personal concept album Whodunnit, Adeline Hotel’s inaugural Lagniappe Session reveals everything on full display. Here, Knishkowy takes on a cut from Richard and Linda Thompson’s 1974 classic I Want To See The Bright Lights Tonight, a country number from Dottie West, and The Bird Calls from his own label, Ruination Records. 

Adeline Hotel :: The Calvary Cross (Richard & Linda Thompson)

If I had to pick one song that is the blueprint for the world I’m trying to build with Adeline Hotel, it is probably this one: the noodly instrumental guitar intro of the original, the meditative dirge with stream of consciousness lyrics, the examination of relationship to self and others, in both abstraction and specificity: I think “I’ll hurt you ’til you need me” is the most cuttingly beautiful lyric that says in 7 words what I belabored over across the entirety of Whodunnit. We stretch out at the end, the way they did live, but not on record, though I read an interview with Linda Thompson about this song recently where she said that “protracted solos are a male fetish,” which made me laugh. Maybe the only cover the Adeline band has routinely performed over the years, because I never get sick of it. 

Adeline Hotel :: What I’m Cut Out To Be (Dottie West)

This song is so strange, seemingly straightforward but with bizarre chord progressions and this tongue in cheek lyrical approach that is actually quite deep. At times, she’s seeking to become what she’s “cut out to be”, and at others, the scissors are what’s actually trimming her down to what someone else wants her to be. Thematically, that felt deeply resonant with the songs I’ve been writing lately. I like how funky we made it relative to the original, and it gave us an opportunity to flex some of the parameters that usually inhabit the AH world.

Adeline Hotel :: Old Faithful (The Bird Calls)

Sam is one of the most prolific and gifted songwriters around these days, and happens to be one of my best friends. I think people should cover songs by their friends more often. Sam’s the kind of friend where we text each other demos right after writing them all the time, so I didn’t realize how intimidating it would be for me to send him a cover of his own song. I first heard this song at the exact point in my life that I needed it, and that chorus lyric still hits me in new ways every time: “strange thing, starting over / strange thing, moving backwards.” His new record Melody Trail is an all timer and not to be missed!
